Reading Internet Reference Models, I learned what they are and what they do. The International Organization for Standardization developed the OSI Model, standardizing communication globally across diverse network devices. It divides network function into seven physical layers, data link, network, transport, session, presentation, and application. Each layer handles specific tasks, such as managing physical connections, routing signals, and ensuring data integrity. Understanding the OSI model is helpful if I were to work with technology or networking. It can assist in troubleshooting network issues, optimizing devices, and communication, and improving problem-solving skills, which can add to the daily use of digital devices.
